Welcome to Your Second Reflection Writing for Advanced Writing

In this assignment, you must reflect on times/instances when you have had to unlearn to learn. Are you ready to begin?

Stage 1: First Draft Submitted
Stage 2: Peer Feedback Received
Stage 3: Revising with Details
Stage 4: Final Submission

You have created your first essay draft...

"You wrote about how you had to learn to appreciate the phrase 'work smarter, not harder.' Your draft gives a generic overview, hoping it would be enough. What do you do next?"

Peer Feedback: OH NO!

"Your professor comments that your piece is lacking in detail. It feels like you're telling the reader rather than showing them. What do you do?"

Reflection After Peer Feedback

"This is a great next step to understand what it means to add details in your work that give it character. By gathering peer feedback and looking at positive examples, you were able to see the detail in effect, showing you how powerful a few extra descriptions can be in creating a connection between the reader and the writer."

Revising with Details

"You read your peers' work and realize what it means to add details that make writing vivid. What details do you focus on?"

Reflection After Revision

Final Submission

"After rewriting and emphasizing your journey, should you seek one last round of feedback or trust your instincts and submit?"

Outcome: Collaboration Wins

By embracing collaboration one more time, you ensured your work is as strong as possible. A small but critical improvement made your argument shine.

Outcome: Confidence Pays Off

Your confidence was validated with a positive reception. Trusting your instincts demonstrated your growth in judgment and writing skill.
